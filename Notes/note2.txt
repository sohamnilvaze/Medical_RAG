Patient ID: 9021 Admission Date: 2145-03-08 Discharge Date: 2145-03-14 Date of Birth: 2095-11-02 Sex: M Service: Pulmonology CHIEF COMPLAINT: Severe cough, fever, and difficulty breathing. HISTORY OF PRESENT ILLNESS: A 49-year-old male presented with progressively worsening cough and high-grade fever for one week. The patient also reported pleuritic chest pain and night sweats. He had no hemoptysis but described significant fatigue and poor oral intake. PAST MEDICAL HISTORY: 1. Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) diagnosed five years ago. 2. Tobacco use: 20 pack-years, quit two years ago. 3. No diabetes or cardiac history. MEDICATIONS ON ADMISSION: Salbutamol inhaler as needed, Tiotropium inhaler once daily. HOSPITAL COURSE: On admission, patient was tachypneic with oxygen saturation of 85% on room air. Chest X-ray demonstrated left lower lobe consolidation. Sputum culture grew Streptococcus pneumoniae. He was started on IV ceftriaxone and azithromycin, and supplemental oxygen at 3 L/min via nasal cannula. Over the next 72 hours, his fever subsided, and oxygen requirement decreased. Repeat labs showed improvement in leukocytosis. He was transitioned to oral amoxicillin-clavulanate. DISCHARGE PLAN: Continue oral antibiotics for seven days. Resume inhaler therapy with Tiotropium and Salbutamol. Pulmonary rehabilitation referral made. Advised vaccination with pneumococcal and influenza vaccines. Counselled on recognition of worsening symptoms (fever, increasing shortness of breath, hemoptysis). Follow-up in pulmonology clinic in two weeks.
